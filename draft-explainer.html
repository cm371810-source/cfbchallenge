The flagship spring event of the CFB Challenge

What if Selection Sunday and the NFL Draft had a college football offseason baby?

That's the Big Ten–SEC Challenge Draft. Every spring, Big Ten and SEC commissioners build the premier non-conference schedule in college football during a nationally televised draft featuring every participating head coach and a returning star player.

16 Challenge matchups created live

Challenge Draft Day

A schedule reveal, selection show and joint media day

Shortly after spring practice concludes, every participating Big Ten and SEC school sends its head coach and one returning star player to Challenge Draft Day.

As each Big Ten–SEC Challenge matchup is announced, both schools take the stage for photographs, interviews and national television coverage. Commissioner strategy becomes part of the show, and every selection immediately creates a season's worth of storylines.

Premium spring inventory

A major college football event when the calendar needs one

National Signing Day has become less dramatic, and spring games no longer command the same attention. The CFB Challenge fills that opening with fresh, live college football inventory during one of the quietest periods of the sports year.

One made-for-television spring event creates sixteen more during Challenge Weekend—and the games carry real postseason weight.

Behind the Draft

How the Big Ten–SEC Challenge is built

Before the draft begins, each conference designates its Standard Bearer. Those programs are paired in the SEC/B1G Commissioners' Classic, the featured matchup of Challenge Weekend, played on Labor Day night.

The SEC/B1G Commissioners' Classic is the showcase event of Challenge Weekend. It always features each conference's chosen Standard Bearer and the weekend's premier matchup. If the Challenge finishes 8–8, the winner of the SEC/B1G Commissioners' Classic is awarded the Challenge Championship as the official tiebreaker. Otherwise, it remains the biggest non-conference game of the opening weekend—a game worthy of the national spotlight regardless of the standings.

With the SEC/B1G Commissioners' Classic established, the commissioners draft the remaining fifteen matchups.

Selection 1
Big Ten
The Big Ten selects the first matchup, played on the selected Big Ten team's field.
Selection 2
SEC
The SEC selects the next matchup, played on the selected SEC team's field.
Selections 3–7
Alternating
The Big Ten and SEC continue alternating selections through the first seven drafted matchups.
Selections 8 & 9
SEC, then SEC
The order swaps at the midpoint, giving the SEC both the eighth and ninth selections.
Selections 10–15
Alternating
The conferences alternate the remaining selections until every Big Ten–SEC Challenge matchup is assigned.
The selecting conference controls the home team and the matchup attached to that home date. Each commissioner must balance conference wins, television value, national interest, rivalry potential, geography and the risk of creating an obvious mismatch.

Challenge Weekend

The crown jewel of the non-conference schedule

Every Big Ten–SEC Challenge game is played during Challenge Weekend, spanning Friday through Labor Day Monday. Together, the sixteen games create one unmistakable national event instead of scattering marquee non-conference matchups across unrelated weeks.

Why the format works

The matchups reflect the teams and storylines that exist now

Traditional non-conference games are often scheduled so far in advance that the programs, coaches and national storylines have changed by kickoff. The Big Ten–SEC Challenge responds to the current landscape.

Better television

Commissioners must build a slate with enough national brands, competitive games and fresh matchups to carry an entire holiday weekend.

Visible strategy

Every selection reveals priorities. Fans can see which programs were protected, challenged, targeted or avoided.

Games that matter

Every result contributes to the Challenge standings, the Challenge Champion and a meaningful postseason reward.

The postseason stakes

The Challenge has real consequences

Under the CFB Challenge postseason model, the winning conference receives an additional automatic berth in the CFB Challenge Play-In Weekend.

That means every game can affect the national championship path. A road upset by a middle-tier program can matter just as much to the final standings as a heavyweight matchup in prime time.

Conference-wide success creates conference-wide opportunity. The extra automatic bid rewards the league that performs best across the full Big Ten–SEC Challenge, giving every participating program a reason to care about every result.

The membership imbalance

What about the other two Big Ten teams?

Because the Big Ten currently has two more members than the SEC, two Big Ten programs each season will not be assigned a Big Ten–SEC Challenge matchup.

Those schools instead host regional Group of Five opponents during Challenge Weekend. These games do not count toward the official Big Ten–SEC Challenge standings but complete each institution's Labor Day weekend schedule.

To ensure no institution is financially disadvantaged by the conference-size imbalance, the Big Ten Conference funds the game guarantee for each participating Group of Five opponent.

The flagship of the CFB Challenge

The draft creates the games. Challenge Weekend shapes the postseason.

The Big Ten–SEC Challenge is the flagship event of the CFB Challenge—a system designed to create better schedules, meaningful non-conference competition and a more compelling path to the national championship. It begins with Challenge Draft Day, culminates in the SEC/B1G Commissioners' Classic and rewards the conference that earns the Challenge Championship on the field.
